Introduction

Thermite is a substance that when activated undergoes a massive exothermic chemical 
reaction called a thermite reaction, usually involving aluminium, iron, and oxygen.
8Al + 3Fe2O3  2Fe3Al + 3Al2O3 [1]
As the Aluminium reduces the Iron, large a quantity of energy is released. This reaction reaches 
temperatures of about 2400°C causing the products of the reaction to melt. [2]
It was discovered in 1895 Hans Goldschmidt, German chemist, who experimented with using 
this reaction to reduce metals to create pure, carbon free products. This process does create 
carbon free metals, but trace amounts of Aluminium can be found instead. [2]
